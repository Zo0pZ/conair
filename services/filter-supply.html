Commercial Kitchen Filter Supply

Regular filter replacement is essential for maintaining grease capture efficiency and reducing fire risk. We supply commercial kitchen filters in all types and sizes across Bristol & Somerset.

Get a QuoteCall 01934 528 450

Commercial kitchen canopy filters are the first line of defence against grease entering the extract ductwork. Blocked or worn filters allow grease to pass into the duct, accelerating build-up and increasing fire risk. Regular filter replacement — combined with scheduled duct cleaning — is the foundation of a safe kitchen ventilation maintenance programme.

✓

Baffle filters — the most common commercial kitchen filter type, made from stainless steel or aluminium, with a labyrinth design for high grease capture efficiency.

✓

Cassette filters — typically used in higher-capacity commercial kitchens and fast food operations where maximum grease capture is required.

✓

Mesh filters — used in lower-duty applications or as secondary filters in some canopy designs.

✓

Custom sizes — we can source filters to non-standard sizes if your canopy requires it.

✓

Replacement advice — we advise on the correct filter type and replacement interval for your specific canopy and cooking application.

✓

Paired with cleaning visits — many clients have filters supplied and replaced as part of their scheduled TR19 cleaning visits.

Filters should be inspected regularly and replaced before they become saturated. Visible grease dripping from filters is a sign of overdue replacement.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should commercial kitchen filters be replaced?

This depends on cooking volume and type. High-volume fryer operations may need filters replacing every week or two; lighter-use kitchens may manage monthly. Filters should be inspected as part of the daily or weekly kitchen check and replaced when they show signs of saturation.

What is the difference between baffle and mesh filters?

Baffle filters use angled metal baffles that cause grease droplets to separate out of the airstream by inertia — they are more effective and are the standard for commercial kitchens. Mesh filters use a fine screen to physically trap droplets — they offer lower resistance but lower efficiency, typically used in lighter-duty applications.

Can you supply filters for older or unusual canopy sizes?

Yes. We can source filters in non-standard sizes for older or bespoke canopies. Send us the dimensions and we will source the correct filter for your canopy.
